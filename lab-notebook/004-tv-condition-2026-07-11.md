# 004 — RQ3 TV condition: wearer speech survives; TV becomes people

**Date:** 2026-07-11 evening (payload 2026-07-12 UTC). Session A-tv-1.

## Conditions

Rainbow Passage (script A) read ~3 m from an iPad playing *The Good Wife*
(dialogue-heavy drama) at slightly above typical conversational volume.
Researcher read from a screen; conversation ended manually in-app.
Payload (TV dialogue redacted as copyrighted broadcast content, structure
preserved): [`../data/samples/payload-002-tv-condition.json`](../data/samples/payload-002-tv-condition.json).

## Result 1 — wearer accuracy under TV interference: WER 0

All wearer segments verbatim against the (amended) reference — the single
word-level deviation was the reader's, not the ASR's (an inserted article,
confirmed by the researcher; reference amended per method rules). Running
tally: **Fieldy is 2-for-2 verbatim on wearer speech** (quiet, tv).
Near-field/far-field separation at this distance and volume is excellent.

## Result 2 — TV dialogue is transcribed as real people [key finding]

The show's dialogue was captured, transcribed, and attributed to
**"Speaker 1" and "Speaker 3"** — indistinguishable in the data model from
humans in the room. 7 of 12 segments in the conversation are fictional
content. Also: **the wearer was labeled "Speaker 2"** — no wearer
designation exists in the payload, and the wearer doesn't even reliably get
the first label slot.

Implications:

- Confirms on Fieldy the TV-contamination failure mode reported for
  comparable wearables (Limitless, Omi) — previously [inferred], now
  [verified] here.
- For lifelog/assistive use, the archive silently accumulates fictional
  conversations presented as real. Any downstream automation (task
  extraction, "what did the doctor say", daily summaries) must assume
  broadcast contamination unless the environment is controlled.
- Diarization itself performed well — voices were cleanly separated — the
  system simply has no concept of "not a person present."
